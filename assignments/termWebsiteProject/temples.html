Boise, Idaho

The plans for the Boise, Idaho temple were announced on March 31, 1982. Ground was broken on December 18 of the same year and the temple was dedicated on the 25 of May 1984. It was the 27th dedicated temple. Its exterior is crafted of white granite from China. Its six spire design mimics that of the Salt Lake City temple, which was built in 1893. Idaho state officials, including the current Governor John Evan toured the temple during its first open house. The temple later underwent renovation and was rededicated in May 1987. Then in 2011 the temple had another major renovation and was dedicated for a third time on November 18, 2012.

Idaho Falls, Idaho

The Idaho Falls temple was announced on March 3, 1937. In December of the same year ground was broken and the temple was dedicated on September 23, 1945, by George Albert Smith. Due to the second World War it took years longer than expected to finish the temple. It was the 8th temple dedicated and the first in Idaho. It was the first temple to feature a centeral spire and was designed by architecht John Fetzer Sr. who was inspired by the design of anchient Nephite temples. The angel Moroni statue wasn't placed on top of the temple until September 1983. On June 4, 2017 the temple was rededicated following a two year renovation.

Twin Falls, Idaho

The Twin Falls temple was announced on the 2nd of October in 2004. Ground was broken two years later on April 15, 2006. It was the 128th temple dedicated. The temple, as well as its custom murals inside are especially inspired by the nearby Shoshone Falls. Its interior also contains a Syringa flower motif that is carried on throughout the entire temple. Materials such as African wood and Indian granite were imported and used to construct the temple. The exterior of the temple is quartz mined from Washington state. On August 24, 2008 the temple was dedicated.

Rexburg, Idaho

On December 12, 2003 the Rexburg, Idaho temple was announced. Ground was broken on July 30, 2005. It is the 125th temple dedicated. The temple is located adjacent to Brigham Young University- Idaho. There is a wheat motif throughout the interior of the temple, which is seen especially in the 700 glass windows and art pieces. The wheat is symbolic of the widespread argricultural industry that is prevalent Rexburg area. The temple is constructed of materials from around the world, including stone and tiles from Israel. During its open house over 200,000 people visited the temple. On February 10, 2008 the temple was dedicated.
